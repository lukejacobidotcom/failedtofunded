02

Widget A — Positioning Edge Gauge

One card per instrument, with a built-in dropdown to switch markets. Shows where profitable traders are positioned vs. unprofitable traders vs. the whole crowd — with a single divergence number that says instantly when the two groups are on opposite sides of the market. Three sizes for any grid slot.

03

Widget B — Market Pulse Strip

The whole futures board at a glance: paired bars per instrument — P for profitable traders, U for unprofitable traders — with a tilt arrow for the profitable side and a Δ divergence badge. Designed to sit directly under the stat tiles — the first thing a trader scans before the next copy session. Hover any cell for detail.

04

Widget C — Intraday Positioning Chart

The deep-dive panel: price candles and cohort positioning on one chart, aligned bar-for-bar, from MarketFramework's continuously-recorded 1m/5m/15m positioning candles. A trader sees in one glance when profitable traders flipped — and how price followed. Hover for the crosshair; switch instrument and timeframe.

Why this wins for Tradesyncer

Session-long stickiness

Today the dashboard is where traders review results after the fact. Live market positioning gives them a reason to keep it open during the session — more time in product, more surface for everything else you ship in the rebuild.

Data nobody else can offer

COT is weekly and institutional. Broker sentiment is unsplit retail flow. This is live funded-trader positioning split by who is actually profitable — sourced from real prop order flow. No other copier, journal, or dashboard has it.

Zero build lift

One script tag or iframe per widget, themed to your tokens (light and dark, as previewed here). JSON REST underneath, edge-cached at 10 seconds, CORS-ready. Your team integrates in an afternoon, not a sprint.

Drop-in embed (proposed API)

<!-- one tag in the dashboard shell --> <script src="https://widgets.marketframework.com/edge.js" defer></script> <!-- then place widgets anywhere --> <mkfm-pulse theme="tradesyncer-light"></mkfm-pulse> <mkfm-gauge symbol="ES" size="md"></mkfm-gauge> <mkfm-history symbol="ES" tf="5m"></mkfm-history>
